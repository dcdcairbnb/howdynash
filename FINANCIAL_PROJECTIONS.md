## Cost Structure

### One-time costs per city

Domain registration: $12 to $40

Logo or brand asset design (optional): $0 to $300

LLC formation (optional, recommended): $300

Trademark registration (optional, recommended for brand): $350 per class

Total one-time per city: $12 to $1000

### Recurring monthly costs

Vercel hosting: $0 (Hobby plan free, Pro is $20/month if needed)

Domain renewal: $1 to $3 per month annualized

Anthropic Claude API: $1 to $30 per month at typical traffic

Google Places API: $0 to $50 per month (first $200/month free)

Foursquare API: $0 (free tier, sufficient)

AviationStack: $0 to $10 per month

Plausible Analytics: $9 per month

Email service (Postmark or similar for forms): $0 to $15 per month

Total recurring per city: $11 to $120 per month

Realistic average: $25 to $50 per month per city

### Costs that do not scale per city

Anthropic API key works for any number of cities (one account).

Foursquare and Google keys work for any number of cities.

Awin, Viator, GetYourGuide accounts work for any city.

Vercel team plan ($20/month) covers unlimited projects.

Plausible base plan covers 10k pageviews across all sites.

So launching 5 cities costs roughly $50 to $200 per month total, not per-city.

## Revenue Paths

### Path 1: Affiliate commissions

Sources and rates.

Viator: 8% of tour booking value. Average tour booking is $50 to $150. Average commission: $4 to $12.

GetYourGuide: 8% same as Viator.

Booking.com via Awin: 25% to 40% of Booking.com's commission, equates to ~4% of booking value. Average hotel night is $150. Average commission: $6.

Airbnb via Awin: ~5% of stay value. Average stay is $400. Average commission: $20.

VRBO via CJ: ~4% of stay. Average commission: $16.

Expedia: 4% to 6%. Average commission: $6 to $9.

Per converted user value.

Tourists who use the app and book something: 10% of users.

Of those, average commission earned: $15 (mix of tours, hotels, activities).

Effective revenue per user: $1.50.

Math at different traffic levels.

100 daily users (3000/month): $4500/month, but conversion lag means realized cash 60-90 days later.

Wait, let me redo with realistic conversion rates.

Affiliate conversion rate from clicks: 2% to 5%.

Click rate from app users: 30% click an affiliate link.

So 100 users → 30 clicks → 1 conversion → $15 commission.

100 daily users (3000/month) = 30 conversions = $450/month at full attribution.

Realistic cash arrival: 30 to 60 days delayed.

10x traffic (1000 daily users) scales linearly to roughly $4500/month.

### Path 2: Hotel white-label partnerships

Pricing model: $200 to $500 per month per hotel.

Pitch: "Your guests get a free Nashville concierge. Your front desk gets fewer 'where to eat' interruptions. You get a branded experience for less than the cost of one part-time employee."

Per hotel revenue.

Boutique hotel: $200 to $300 per month.

Mid-tier hotel chain: $300 to $500 per month.

Luxury hotel: $500 to $1000 per month with white-label branding.

Per city ceiling.

Nashville has 200+ hotels. Realistically pitch the top 50.

If 10 sign at $300 average: $3000/month per city.

If 20 sign at $300 average: $6000/month per city.

This is the largest revenue path. B2B is where the real money is.

Sales cycle: 4 to 12 weeks per hotel. Slow but high-value.

### Path 3: Sponsored local listings

Local restaurants, distilleries, tour operators pay to be featured.

Pricing: $50 to $200 per month per business.

Per city ceiling.

Realistic: 20 paying businesses at $100 average: $2000/month.

Aggressive: 50 paying at $150 average: $7500/month.

Easier sell than hotels but lower per-customer revenue.

Sales cycle: 2 to 4 weeks per business.

### Path 4: Display ads

Banner ads from Google AdSense or local sponsors.

Revenue: $1 to $5 per 1000 page views.

At 100 daily users (3000/month, ~10000 page views): $10 to $50 per month.

Not material until traffic is high.

Compromises UX. Skip until other paths are exhausted.

### Path 5: Lead generation

Sell qualified leads to local businesses.

User searches "honky tonk pub crawl" → sell that lead to a tour operator.

Revenue: $5 to $50 per qualified lead.

Math: 100 daily users → 10 leads per day → $100/day average → $3000/month.

Requires manual setup. Not turnkey.

### Path 6: Premium subscription

Free with limits, paid for unlimited.

Pricing: $5/month for premium.

Conversion rate: 1% to 3% of users at most.

Per 1000 daily users: 10 to 30 paying subscribers = $50 to $150/month.

Not significant until very high traffic.

### Path 7: Acquisition (long shot)

Tourism conglomerate buys you for $50k to $500k.

Probability: 1 to 5% in a 3 year window.

Worth pursuing only if you build genuine traffic and brand.

## Breakeven Analysis

### Per-city breakeven

Monthly cost: $50.

Need to generate $50/month to break even.

That is 35 affiliate conversions, OR one $50 sponsored listing, OR 1/4 of one hotel partnership.

Achievable in months 2 to 6 with consistent marketing.

### Time to first revenue

Affiliate clicks: immediate. First commission: 30 to 90 days after first booking.

Hotel partnership: 4 to 12 weeks of pitching to first signed deal.

Sponsored listings: 2 to 4 weeks of cold outreach to first paid listing.

Realistic first dollar: month 2 or 3.

### Per-city revenue scenarios

Pessimistic. 50 daily users, 1 hotel partnership: $200/month.

Realistic. 200 daily users, 5 hotel partnerships, 5 sponsored listings: $2500/month.

Optimistic. 500 daily users, 15 hotel partnerships, 20 sponsored listings, $5000+ affiliate: $12,000/month.

### Per-city profit (revenue minus costs)

Pessimistic: $150/month profit.

Realistic: $2450/month profit.

Optimistic: $11,950/month profit.

## Multi-City Economics

5 cities, all operating, all at realistic scenario.

Revenue: 5 × $2500 = $12,500/month.

Costs: $200 (shared infra) + 5 × $25 (per-city) = $325/month.

Profit: $12,175/month or roughly $146,000/year.

10 cities at realistic scenario.

Revenue: 10 × $2500 = $25,000/month.

Costs: $400 total.

Profit: $24,600/month or roughly $295,000/year.

Sales effort scales linearly. To realistically support 10 cities you need:

Either spend full-time on it.

Or hire one person to handle hotel sales and content updates ($60k/year salary).

After hiring: $295k - $60k = $235k profit.

## What Could Go Wrong

Risk 1. Hotels reject the pitch. Need to find product-market fit on B2B. Mitigation: test with 10 hotels before scaling to other cities.

Risk 2. Affiliate revenue stays small. Without traffic, affiliate is a trickle. Mitigation: invest in SEO and PWA to drive organic traffic.

Risk 3. LLM costs spike. If a single user starts spamming, costs could balloon. Mitigation: rate limiting, message length caps, conversation length caps.

Risk 4. API providers change pricing. Foursquare, Google could remove free tiers. Mitigation: build provider-agnostic so you can swap.

Risk 5. Visit Music City builds something competitive. The official tourism board has resources. Mitigation: move faster, partner with local hotels, build moats.

Risk 6. You burn out. Solo founder fatigue is real. Mitigation: only expand to new cities if Nashville is generating real revenue.

## When to Quit

Quit if all of these are true at month 6.

Less than 50 daily users despite marketing effort.

Zero hotel partnerships signed despite pitching 30+.

Total revenue under $200/month.

You stopped enjoying the work.

A better opportunity surfaces.

Pivot if:

Users love the chat but affiliate conversion is low. Maybe pivot to subscription.

Hotels love the demo but won't pay $200/month. Maybe pivot to per-booking commission.

The LLM is the killer feature. Maybe pivot to "build your own city app" platform.

Continue if month 6 shows:

100+ daily users.

1+ hotel partnership signed.

$300+ monthly revenue.

You still find it interesting.
